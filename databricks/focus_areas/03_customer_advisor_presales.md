## SA 角度

這題不要講成「我辦了 seminar」。要講成：

- 你理解 VP 想推 AI mindset 的需求。
- 你理解同事的 resistance：忙、覺得自己的 use case 不夠厲害。
- 你幫 speaker shape content。
- 你經過 VP / senior reviewer review。
- 你把 AI discussion 從 demo 拉到 workflow / product impact。
- 你能跟不同層級的人溝通。

## Opening STAR 口說稿

> One example of customer advisor or stakeholder influence is the GenAI seminar series I helped lead in 2023.
>
> At that time, our VP believed AI would change how our department worked, but most people were still unsure what AI could actually do. I was selected to host a bi-weekly seminar series for over 100 people. My role was to find speakers, shape the content, explore the boundary of AI at that time, and go through reviews with our VP and senior reviewers.
>
> The difficult part was not only technical. Many engineers were busy, and some felt their AI experiments were too simple to present. I had to understand their concerns, help them shape the story, and make the content useful for the audience.
>
> Some ideas later became real workflows, including an internal knowledge Q&A chatbot for development and DevOps knowledge. More importantly, the seminar helped the department build the habit of experimenting with AI early.
>
> I think this maps well to pre-sales or customer advisory work. You need to understand what different stakeholders care about, help them frame the problem, communicate the solution clearly, and move the group toward action.

## 追問準備

如果問「你怎麼影響 VP / senior stakeholders」：

> I tried to translate technical experiments into business or workflow value. With senior stakeholders, I usually avoid going too deep into implementation first. I focus on why the problem matters, what changes in the workflow, and what kind of impact we can expect.
