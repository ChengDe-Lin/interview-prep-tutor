## SA 角度

這題不要講成「我辦了 seminar」。要講成：

- 你理解 VP 想推 AI mindset 的需求。
- 你理解同事的 resistance：忙、覺得自己的 use case 不夠厲害。
- 你幫 speaker shape content。
- 你經過 VP / senior reviewer review。
- 你把 AI discussion 從 demo 拉到 workflow / product impact。
- 你能跟不同層級的人溝通。
- 你也幾次代表部門向副董事長報告技術細節和規劃，證明 leadership trust 你能把複雜技術轉成高層能決策的語言。

這題建議用兩層 proof：

1. Seminar：證明你能 influence broad audience，理解不同 stakeholder 的 resistance，推動 adoption mindset。
2. 副董事長報告：證明你能做 executive-level technical advisory，把架構、roadmap、risk、impact 講到可以影響決策。

開場要誠實：

- 你過去沒有很多直接 external customer-facing / pre-sales 經驗。
- 但你有對內 stakeholder advisory 和 executive communication 經驗。
- 某種程度上，說服高層相信一個 technical direction，和說服 customer 相信一個 solution，有相似的能力底層：理解對方關心什麼、建立信任、講清楚 trade-off、把技術轉成決策。
- 高層 presentation 給你的最大訓練是：每一句話都要先預判對方會怎麼理解、會追問什麼、會擔心什麼，避免把討論帶到不利或難解釋的方向。

## Opening STAR 口說稿

> To be honest, I have not had many direct external customer-facing or pre-sales experiences in my past role. Most of my experience was internal. But I do have several experiences advising internal stakeholders and senior leadership, and I think some of the underlying skills are similar: understanding what people care about, building trust, explaining trade-offs clearly, and helping them believe in a technical direction.
>
> One example is the GenAI seminar series I owned in 2023.
>
> At that time, our VP believed AI would change how our department worked, but most people were still unsure what AI could actually do. I was selected to host a bi-weekly seminar series for over 100 people. My role was to find speakers, shape the content, explore the boundary of AI at that time, and go through reviews with our VP and senior reviewers.
>
> The difficult part was not only technical. Many engineers were busy, and some felt their AI experiments were too simple to present. I had to understand their concerns, help them shape the story, and make the content useful for the audience.
>
> Some ideas later became real workflows, including an internal knowledge Q&A chatbot for development and DevOps knowledge. More importantly, the seminar helped the department build the habit and the mindset of experimenting with AI early.
>
> Another signal is that I was also trusted several times to represent my department and present technical details and planning to vice chairman of ASUS. Those discussions were different from engineering design reviews. I had to explain the architecture, the trade-offs, the expected impact, and the roadmap in a way that supported leadership decision-making.
>
> The biggest lesson I learned from those executive presentations is that every sentence needs to be intentional. Before I say something, I need to think about how the other person may interpret it, what concern it may trigger, and what follow-up question they may ask. It is not about hiding problems. It is about communicating clearly enough that the discussion moves toward the right decision, instead of creating confusion or opening a direction that does not help the decision.
>
> I think this maps well to pre-sales or customer advisory work. You need to understand what different stakeholders care about, help them frame the problem, communicate the solution clearly, and move the group toward action. Sometimes the audience is a broad engineering group, and sometimes it is an executive decision maker. The communication style is different, but the goal is similar: make the problem, options, risks, and next step clear enough for people to make a decision.

## 追問準備

如果問「你怎麼影響 VP / senior stakeholders」：

> I tried to translate technical experiments into business or workflow value. With senior stakeholders, I usually avoid going too deep into implementation first. I focus on why the problem matters, what changes in the workflow, and what kind of impact we can expect.

如果問「seminar 跟 pre-sales / customer advisor 有什麼關係」：

> I would not say it is the same as external pre-sales, and I want to be transparent about that. But the core motion is similar: understand the audience, identify what blocks adoption, shape the story, explain trade-offs, and move people from curiosity to action. In my case, the audience was internal engineers and executives, but the advisory skill is transferable.

如果問「你怎麼跟 executive 溝通技術」：

> I usually start from the decision they need to make, not from the implementation details. I also think carefully about every sentence before I say it: how they may interpret it, what concern it may trigger, and what follow-up question they may ask. Then I explain the problem, options, trade-offs, risks, and expected impact in a way that helps the decision move forward.
