# GovTech — Singapore Research

## Company Overview

- **What it is**: Government Technology Agency of Singapore — the central government agency responsible for building and running Singapore's digital infrastructure and public-facing digital services.
- **Size**: Large public-sector tech organisation with hundreds of engineers, data scientists, DevOps engineers, and UX/UI designers.
- **Sub-units relevant to engineers**:
  - **Open Government Products (OGP)** — a lean, startup-like unit inside GovTech that builds citizen-facing digital products fast (e.g., FormSG, HealthHub, Parking.sg). Engineers here enjoy significant autonomy and are closest to product-engineering culture.
  - **GovTech Consulting** — embedded teams deployed to government agencies for modernisation projects (e.g., Maritime Port Authority Digital Twin).
  - **AI Programme** — strategic initiative to scale AI adoption Whole-Of-Government (WOG); actively hiring Full Stack engineers.
  - **Singapore Government Tech Stack (SGTS) team** — builds the shared platform (SHIP-HATS, SGTS, Government on Commercial Cloud) that 200+ systems across 40+ agencies rely on.
- **Core products/services**:
  - Singpass — 4.2M+ users, 1,700+ services, ~300M transactions/year
  - MyInfo — citizen digital-data platform that pre-fills government forms
  - SGDS (Singapore Government Design System) — shared frontend component library
  - Analytics.gov — ML/data analytics platform serving 1,600+ users across 80 agencies
  - RedeemSG, FormSG, Parking.sg, HealthHub (OGP products)

## Engineering Culture

- **Core values**: **ABC — Agile, Bold, Collaborative**. Interview questions explicitly test alignment with these values.
- **Tech stack**:
  - Backend: Java, Node.js, Python
  - Frontend: React, TypeScript, SGDS component library (built on Web Components)
  - Cloud: Government on Commercial Cloud (GCC) — primarily AWS with some Azure
  - DevOps: SHIP-HATS (CI/CD pipeline built on GitLab), Docker, Kubernetes
  - Data/AI: Python, machine learning frameworks, Analytics.gov platform
  - Key tooling: Developer CLI for automating setup and daily tasks; standardised templates and shared libraries
- **Known engineering values**:
  - Designing for public impact at scale (every product touches millions of citizens)
  - Security and compliance are first-class concerns (government-grade)
  - Iterative, fast-ship approach — MyInfo's launch time cut from 1 year to 4 months via SGTS
  - Open source where possible (most OGP code is on GitHub under GovTechSG)
- **Work style**:
  - HQ teams (OGP, AI Programme, SGTS): modern, agile, autonomy-heavy
  - Agency-embedded (Consulting) roles: more traditional, governed by agency's own culture
  - Culture varies significantly by team — ask specifically which product group you'll join
  - Hybrid work; government organisations are generally 3–4 days in office

## Customized Pitch Notes

- **What to emphasize in "Why Here"**:
  - Genuine connection to public impact at scale — GovTech's products touch every Singaporean; frame your work as wanting to build infrastructure that matters beyond profit
  - NTU background: signal you are embedded in Singapore's tech ecosystem and understand the mission
  - AI experience aligns perfectly with the AI Programme's current hiring push — mention the agentic AI harness directly
  - Micro-frontend / platform thinking maps to SGDS and SGTS shared-platform work — name-drop these if you've researched them
- **What to watch out for**:
  - GovTech is a government agency — avoid framing that sounds dismissive of process/compliance. Show you can operate boldly *within* constraints.
  - Team culture varies widely — ask clearly in interviews which sub-unit the role sits in (OGP vs. Consulting vs. HQ product team) as the experience differs dramatically
  - Bureaucracy is real: the "Bold" value is there precisely because bureaucracy is the default. Show you push back constructively.
  - Online assessment is proctored — be prepared for a strict environment
