# Meta — Singapore Research

## Company Overview
- Singapore office is a full-stack engineering hub, not a satellite office — teams own major product areas with global impact
- Two primary engineering organizations:
  - **Commerce Engineering**: Owns Meta's hardware device commerce lifecycle (discovery, purchase, warranties, returns, support) — user base in the millions
  - **Ads & Business Platform**: Includes Business Integrity Review (tooling to catch bad advertisers), Business Support Platform (support experience across all Meta business products)
- Notable: Meta acquired **Manus**, a Singapore-based AI agent startup, for ~$2B in December 2025 — Manus continues operating from Singapore
- Strict RTO policy: 3 days/week minimum in office

## Engineering Culture
- **Tech stack**: React, GraphQL, Relay (frontend); Hack (PHP variant), Python, C++ (backend); PyTorch (ML/AI); Kotlin/Java (Android — 10M+ lines of Kotlin)
- **"Move Fast"** is the core ethos — "Done is better than perfect"; shipping to users beats perfect engineering
- **Bootcamp program**: New engineers aren't pre-assigned to teams; they rotate and self-select their team after onboarding — rare autonomy in big tech
- Engineers are expected to own projects end-to-end, including cross-functional impact
- Open-source culture: Meta invented React, GraphQL, PyTorch — engineers are expected to think beyond team boundaries
- 2025 strategic focus: AI leadership — massive infra investment ($115–135B capex planned for 2026), ML engineering hiring surge

## Recent News (2025-2026)

- **March 2026**: Reports of potential 20% global headcount reduction (~15,000+ jobs) to offset AI infrastructure spending; Meta has not officially confirmed
- **2025**: Meta Superintelligence Labs launched; AI agents and Llama open-source models are major bets

## Interview Style

### Process Structure
1. Online Assessment (new as of 2025)
2. Recruiter call
3. Technical phone screen (coding)
4. Onsite loop (4 rounds):
   - 1x Coding round
   - 1x AI-enabled coding round (new addition)
   - 1x System Design or Product Architecture
   - 1x Behavioral (dedicated round)

### Known BQ Themes (Meta-specific)
- Conflict resolution with peers, managers, or cross-functional teams
- Growth mindset — receiving and acting on feedback
- Navigating ambiguity — bringing structure where there is none
- Driving progress despite obstacles
- Cross-functional collaboration and influence
- E5 expects: stories where you owned projects impacting 3+ people, proactive initiatives from conception to completion

### Technical Expectations
- Strong LeetCode-style coding (medium/hard)
- System design at scale (Meta scale = hundreds of millions of users)
- AI/ML system design increasingly relevant for 2025+ hiring cycles
- Code quality and communication during live coding

## Customized Pitch Notes

**What to emphasize in "Why Here" section:**
- Meta's acquisition of Manus shows they value Singapore-based AI agent innovation — tie your agentic AI work directly to this thesis
- Commerce Engineering or Ads teams: emphasize experience building systems at scale used by millions
- Meta's open-source culture aligns with sharing knowledge (GenAI seminars)
- Express genuine excitement about building at Meta-scale (hundreds of millions of users) after working on products with significant but smaller footprints

**What to watch out for:**
- Meta E5 behavioral bar is high — every story must show you owned something that impacted 3+ people or cross-team scope; avoid stories that sound like individual contributor work only
- The 2026 layoff uncertainty is real — do not bring it up, but be prepared if asked about company stability concerns
- "Move Fast" culture means low tolerance for over-engineering narratives — frame your technical decisions as "shipped and learned" not "designed perfectly"
- Avoid overly academic framing (despite NTU credentials) — Meta values practical shipping over theory
