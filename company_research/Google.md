## Engineering Culture
- **Tech stack**: C++, Java, Kotlin, Python, Go (Golang), TypeScript; Protobuf and Stubby for internal RPC; strict language style guides enforced across codebases
- Go is a first-class language at Google — the majority of Google Production is managed by systems written in Go; SRE teams especially rely on Go for its balance of performance and readability
- Strong **design-doc culture**: major decisions require written design documents reviewed by peers before implementation
- **Monorepo** codebase with code review gates; emphasis on clean, readable, testable code
- SRE (Site Reliability Engineering) as a discipline originated at Google — low on-call burden relative to industry; reliability and operational excellence are deeply embedded values
- Generous GPU resource allocation for teams building AI features; Gemini integrated into developer tooling
- AI is a top company-wide priority — teams are strongly incentivized to incorporate AI wherever possible
- Engineering-driven organization where technical excellence and promotion go hand-in-hand

## Interview Style
- **Process structure** (as of 2025):
  1. Recruiter screen (30 min)
  2. Phone/online coding screen (45–60 min, algorithmic)
  3. Onsite: 4 rounds — 2–3 technical coding/algorithms, 1 system design (senior+), 1 Googleyness & Leadership (behavioral)
- **Coding rounds**: LeetCode medium-to-hard difficulty; emphasis on time/space complexity, clean code, and communication of thought process
- **System design**: Expected for L5+; tests ability to design scalable distributed systems; must discuss tradeoffs explicitly
- **Googleyness & Leadership round**: Dedicated 45-min behavioral assessment; evaluates cultural fit and leadership potential
- **Known BQ themes (Googleyness)**:
  - Thriving in ambiguity
  - Valuing and responding to feedback
  - Challenging the status quo
  - Doing the right thing / ethical decision-making
  - Collaborative spirit and helping others
  - Ownership and high standards
  - Creative problem-solving
- STAR method expected for all behavioral responses
- Red flags: arrogance, blaming teammates, inability to handle ambiguity
- Hiring committee makes final decision (not individual interviewers)

## Customized Pitch Notes
- **Why Here**: Lead with Google's February 2026 Singapore expansion — specifically the AI Centre of Excellence for Security and the DeepMind lab. Frame your agentic AI harness experience as directly relevant to the problems these teams are working on. Mention the Google Cloud Singapore Engineering Center if targeting cloud-facing product roles.
- **Emphasize**: The combination of (1) architectural leadership (micro-frontend), (2) agentic AI hands-on experience, and (3) academic rigor (NTU 1st) maps cleanly to Google's current Singapore priorities.
- **Googleyness angle**: Prepare stories that show you challenge the status quo (agentic AI harness was non-obvious), thrive in ambiguity (micro-frontend migration), and uplift others (GenAI seminars, collaboration award).
- **Watch out for**: Google interviewers will probe for intellectual humility — avoid any language that sounds like you single-handedly solved everything. Always credit the team and frame your contributions precisely.
- **System design prep**: For L5, expect to design distributed systems at scale. Practice designing systems like YouTube recommendation engine, Google Maps, or large-scale data pipelines.
- **Do not skip**: Quantify everything. Google's hiring committee scrutinizes impact metrics. Prepare exact numbers for every story (latency improvements, user growth, code reduction %, etc.).
