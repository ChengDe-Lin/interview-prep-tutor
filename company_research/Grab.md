## Interview Style

- **Process structure** (well-documented via Grab engineering blog):
  1. **Recruiter phone screen** (~30 min): Background, motivation, culture/4H values alignment
  2. **Online coding assessment** (Codility): 2-3 algorithmic problems, easy-to-medium LeetCode difficulty
  3. **Two back-to-back technical rounds** (~2 hours total, 1 hour each — live coding):
     - **Round 1**: Data Structures & Algorithms (medium-to-hard LeetCode)
     - **Round 2**: Architecture & System Design (distributed systems at SEA scale)
  4. **Culture / final round**: Past projects deep-dive + cultural fit assessment (4H values)
- **Known BQ themes** (4H-aligned):
  - **Heart**: Examples of going beyond your immediate role to help teammates or customers
  - **Hunger**: Self-initiated improvements, learning new tech, data-driven decision-making
  - **Honour**: Keeping commitments under pressure; transparency when things go wrong
  - **Humility**: How you receive feedback; what you've learned from failures
  - Cross-functional collaboration stories are very important
  - "Tell me about an impressive project you know inside and out" — prepare one flagship project
- **Technical expectations**:
  - Algorithms: medium-hard LeetCode; Go or Python preferred but language-agnostic
  - System design: ride-hailing scale (millions of concurrent requests, real-time geo, eventual consistency), event-driven architectures, microservices
  - Cultural fit is **explicitly** a dealbreaker — Grab will reject technically strong candidates who don't fit the 4H values
- **Difficulty**: Very selective; described as "failing most engineers who go through it." Both technical and cultural bars are high.

## Customized Pitch Notes

- **What to emphasize in "Why Here"**:
  - AI COE is the clearest opening: the agentic AI harness you built maps 1:1 to what Grab's COE is doing with its in-house LLM kit. Name this explicitly — "I saw Grab launched its AI Centre of Excellence and is building internal LLM developer tooling. That's almost exactly what I built."
  - Micro-frontend architecture for super-app scale: Grab's consumer app is the canonical use case for micro-frontends (independent teams, independent deployments). Pitch your architecture experience directly.
  - 4H alignment: prepare one concise sentence for each of the 4Hs backed by a story — interviewers explicitly test these.
  - Robotaxi / future bets: If you have any interest in autonomous systems or ML platform, mention this as a reason Grab is exciting long-term.
  - SEA mission resonates personally as a Singapore-based engineer.
- **What to watch out for**:
  - Cultural fit is a hard filter — a technically flawless candidate who doesn't demonstrate 4H values will be rejected. Rehearse the 4H framing explicitly.
  - Golang is the primary backend language; if your primary language is TypeScript/Java, be ready to discuss your Go fluency or demonstrate willingness to ramp up quickly.
  - System design at Grab scale involves real-time geo, ride dispatch, payment consistency — study these domains specifically.
  - Interview is very selective; expect the process to feel rigorous. Treat each round as a filter, not a formality.
  - "Tell me about an impressive project" question is guaranteed — prepare a deep, end-to-end walkthrough of your best project (likely the agentic AI harness or micro-frontend platform).
