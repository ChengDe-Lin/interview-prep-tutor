# Coinbase — Singapore Research

## Company Overview

- **Singapore office size**: ~70 employees in Singapore (part of 600+ APAC headcount); actively growing
- **Office type**: Engineering Hub launched in Nov 2024 in partnership with the Singapore Economic Development Board (EDB)
- **What teams are based here**: Trading systems (IntX product), Consumer Foundations (retail app reliability/performance), Wallet, Custody, and APAC-focused Web3 teams; internship pipeline with summer 2026 cohort active
- **Regulatory status**: Holds a Major Payment Institution (MPI) license from MAS — one of ~15-20 firms still fully compliant after the June 2025 MAS tightening
- **Core products/services relevant to Singapore**:
  - Coinbase Business — launched Nov 2025 as the *first international market* outside the US; serves Singapore SMBs with USDC payments, global payouts, SGD stablecoin rails via Standard Chartered partnership
  - Retail exchange (coinbase.com) and Coinbase Wallet
  - Coinbase Custody for institutional clients in APAC

## Engineering Culture

- **Tech stack**:
  - Backend: Go (Golang), gRPC, Python
  - Frontend/Mobile: TypeScript, React, React Native (shared iOS/Android codebase)
  - Data: PostgreSQL, Redis, Kafka, Time-series DB
  - Infrastructure: AWS, microservices architecture
  - Web3: Solidity, EVM, Base L2 chain, node infrastructure via Coinbase Cloud
- **Known engineering values**:
  - "Act Like an Owner" — engineers take 100% responsibility beyond their job title
  - Nine cultural tenets: Clear Communication, Efficient Execution, Act Like an Owner, Top Talent, Championship Team, Continuous Learning, Customer Focus, Repeatable Innovation, Positive Energy
  - Mission-first, high-performance culture; apolitical and focused on crypto mission
- **Work style**:
  - Remote-friendly (many Singapore roles listed as "Remote - Singapore")
  - Final interview loop is 4x 60-min one-on-one rounds including a 30-min behavioral with hiring manager
  - Strong emphasis on crypto domain knowledge in technical rounds

## Interview Style

- **Process structure** (6-8 weeks typical):
  1. Recruiter phone screen (background + interest in crypto)
  2. Online technical assessment or take-home
  3. Virtual final loop: 4x 60-min rounds
     - Domain knowledge rounds (role-dependent — e.g., trading systems, backend infra)
     - 30-min behavioral round with hiring manager
  4. Possible work trial / practical assignment for some roles
- **Known BQ themes**:
  - Dealing with ambiguity and incomplete information
  - Learning new technology quickly
  - Advocating for user needs vs. business goals
  - Ownership under failure — "Tell me about a time you failed"
  - Cross-team collaboration and difficult team member situations
- **Technical expectations**:
  - Algorithms/data structures (LeetCode medium-hard)
  - System design (distributed systems, real-time data, payment/trading infra)
  - Blockchain/crypto domain knowledge is a strong differentiator
  - For Singapore roles: APAC product context and regulatory awareness appreciated

## Customized Pitch Notes

- **What to emphasize in Why Here**:
  - Singapore is Coinbase's first international expansion market for Coinbase Business — joining now means being part of building out APAC infrastructure from scratch, which is rare for a crypto company of this scale
  - Coinbase's MPI license positions it as one of the only compliant crypto exchanges in Singapore — you want to be at the company that's playing the long game on regulation
  - Tie your agentic AI / vibe-coding harness work to Coinbase's onchain economy mission: the future of finance is programmable, and you're building the tooling that makes programmable systems faster to develop
- **What to watch out for**:
  - Expect deep crypto/blockchain knowledge questions — brush up on L1/L2 mechanics, stablecoin design, and MAS regulatory context
  - Coinbase culture is explicitly apolitical and mission-focused; avoid framing your answers in purely social/ESG terms; tie everything back to mission execution and economic freedom
  - The "Act Like an Owner" tenet is tested behaviorally — have stories where you took action outside your formal remit
  - Singapore engineering team is still small (~70 people); demonstrate comfort with ambiguity and building in a scrappy environment
