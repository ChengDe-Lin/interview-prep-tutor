# Palantir — Singapore Research

## Company Overview

- **Singapore office**: Active commercial and government-facing office; Palantir Singapore Pte. Ltd. registered entity; encourages Singapore citizens to apply; in-person/hybrid work model
- **Office size**: Small (~6 open roles as of Feb 2026 per Indeed); Palantir offices are intentionally lean — small teams, high leverage
- **What teams are based here**: Forward Deployed Software Engineers (FDSEs), Solution Specialists, AI Architects, and legal/compliance; the Singapore office is primarily a *deployment and operations* hub rather than a pure product engineering hub
- **APAC go-to-market**: FPT Software is Palantir's exclusive resale partner in Singapore, Vietnam, and Malaysia — commercial expansion via partners; Palantir Asia expected to account for up to 40% of overall sales
- **Core products/services relevant to Singapore**:
  - **Palantir Foundry**: Enterprise data operations platform (analytics, visualization, operational decision-making)
  - **Palantir AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform)**: AI agents and LLM integration over the Ontology; key growth product in 2025-2026
  - **Palantir Gotham**: Government/defense intelligence analytics (classified use cases)
  - **Commercial clients in APAC**: Hyundai, Sompo Insurance, AirAsia, Fujitsu, Doosan have deployed Foundry
  - **Healthcare/Government**: Global healthcare AI contracts active (Joint Commission, OneMedNet); Singapore government sector is a known target market

## Interview Style

- **Process structure** (~36 days average for FDSE):
  1. Recruiter phone screen (20 min): Background, why Palantir, which role (SWE vs. FDSE)
  2. Online assessment (90 min): 2 problems — one algorithms/data ingestion question, one systems design requiring a modular diagram
  3. Virtual onsite (3-5 rounds):
     - **Decomposition interview**: Problem breakdown and structured reasoning — Palantir's signature round; tests how you think, not just what you produce
     - Coding round (algorithms, medium-hard)
     - System design / Re-engineering round
     - Behavioral round (values alignment, mission fit, ethical reasoning)
  4. For FDSE: additional client scenario round assessing communication and stakeholder empathy
- **Known BQ themes** (values: Ownership, Intellectual Rigor, Mission-Driven Focus):
  - "Tell me about a time you made a difficult ethical decision at work"
  - "How do you think about the balance between privacy and security?"
  - "Describe a project where you considered end-user impact of your technical decisions"
  - "Why do you want to do socially impactful work?"
  - "Tell me about a time you took ownership of something outside your formal responsibility"
  - Scenario-based technical questions with user impact framing (not pure LeetCode)
- **Technical expectations**:
  - The **Decomposition interview** is unique to Palantir — practice breaking ambiguous, large-scale problems into structured components; explain your thinking step-by-step
  - System design emphasis: distributed data pipelines, real-time analytics, data sovereignty
  - For FDSE: client-facing communication under pressure, translating technical complexity for non-technical stakeholders
  - Palantir reportedly fails "the vast majority" of candidates — selective bar, especially for FDSEs
