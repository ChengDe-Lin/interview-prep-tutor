# Databricks — Singapore Research

## Company Overview

- **Singapore role**: Databricks named Singapore as its Asia Pacific & Japan (APJ) regional hub in August 2024, in partnership with the Singapore Economic Development Board (EDB). This is the most strategically important non-US office.
- **Headcount**: Databricks more than doubled its Singapore headcount to over 100 employees within two years (announced Sep 2023), with continued aggressive hiring planned.
- **Teams based in Singapore**: Field engineering, professional services, strategy & operations, learning & enablement, and software engineering roles (ML OSS, fullstack). Active Singapore-based SWE job postings include the ML OSS Ecosystem Team (training, deploying, monitoring GenAI agents at scale) and fullstack engineering.
- **Core products/services**: Databricks Data Intelligence Platform, Apache Spark, Delta Lake, MLflow, Unity Catalog, Mosaic AI, Agent Bricks (launched 2025), Lakebase (transactional database, 2025).

## Engineering Culture

- **Tech stack**: Scala (primary for data processing/runtime), Python (ML, data science, notebooks), C++ (high-performance data path), Jsonnet (config management), Protobuf, Bazel (monorepo build tool for Scala, Python, C++, Groovy, Docker, Protobuf). SQL is a first-class interface throughout the platform.
- **Open source first**: Databricks is the company behind Apache Spark (original creators), Delta Lake, MLflow, and Koalas. Engineers contribute to and maintain large open-source communities. In 2025, Databricks donated Declarative Pipelines to Apache Spark.
- **Engineering values**: Ownership, customer obsession, transparency, shipping fast, handling ambiguity. Engineers are expected to work across the full stack (infra, platform, product) and contribute to open-source.
- **Work style**: High-autonomy, fast-moving startup culture despite scale (~7,000+ employees globally). Monorepo discipline, strong CI/CD culture. Singapore engineers are expected to collaborate with US teams across time zones.
- **AI-native**: At the 2025 Data + AI Summit, launched Agent Bricks (agentic AI dev platform), Lakebase, and Databricks One (no-code AI BI). The company's AI products exceeded $1B revenue run-rate in 2025.

## Interview Style

- **Process**: 1-hour recruiter screen → 1 technical phone screen → 4-panel onsite (or virtual): 2x LeetCode/DSA, 1x System Design, 1x Behavioral/Cross-functional. Total timeline: 1–3 months.
- **Behavioral round**: 60-minute session with hiring manager. Topics include: ownership in past projects, handling ambiguity, stakeholder communication, cross-functional collaboration, and conflict resolution. Closely tied to Databricks' core values.
- **Known BQ themes**: "Tell me about a time you led a difficult project," "Describe a time you collaborated with a team with differing opinions," "Talk about a time you had trouble communicating with stakeholders," "Why Databricks?"
- **Technical expectations**: Strong DSA (LeetCode medium-hard), distributed systems knowledge, familiarity with Spark/data engineering concepts is a plus for Singapore roles. System design must cover large-scale data and/or ML systems.
- **Culture fit emphasis**: Databricks specifically assesses alignment with ownership, customer obsession, and transparency in high-impact environments.

## Customized Pitch Notes

**What to emphasize in Why Here:**
- Databricks is the company that literally created Apache Spark and Delta Lake — the open-source data infrastructure the industry runs on. The opportunity to work at the APJ hub in Singapore, closest to where data-intensive APAC enterprises are adopting the platform, is unique.
- Explicitly connect your agentic AI harness work to Databricks' Agent Bricks platform — this is a live product area they are actively building in Singapore.
- Mention the IPO trajectory: joining now means being part of the growth story before the public market chapter.
- Reference the Synapxe/Singapore public healthcare data initiative if you want to signal interest in the local impact angle.

**What to watch out for:**
- Databricks engineers are expected to have genuine passion for open source and data infrastructure — avoid positioning yourself purely as a product/frontend engineer. Lead with AI platform engineering and data systems experience.
- The Singapore office is still relatively small (~100 people); expect to work closely with US leadership and manage async communication across time zones. Be ready to discuss how you work in distributed teams.
- LeetCode preparation is mandatory — Databricks is known for rigorous algorithmic rounds even for senior candidates.
- Be ready for "Why Databricks specifically?" with a substantive answer — generic answers about growth or AI will not stand out.
