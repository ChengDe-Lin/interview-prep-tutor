## Engineering Culture
- **Tech stack**: Java (dominant), Python, C++, Go, TypeScript/JavaScript; internally uses Brazil (build system), Crux (code review), Code (Git-based VCS), Pipelines (CI/CD with up to 4 stages: beta/gamma/prod/local)
- **Microservices-first architecture**: every team owns independent services with API-driven design; polyglot persistence (DynamoDB, Aurora, S3, Redshift etc.)
- AWS-first infrastructure: all new projects NAWS (Native AWS); legacy migrating to AWS (MAWS)
- **Two-pizza team** model: small, autonomous teams own services end-to-end; decisions made close to the problem
- Engineering culture is heavily **ownership-driven** — "that's not my job" is culturally unacceptable
- **Bar Raiser** program: every interview loop includes a specially trained Bar Raiser who ensures hiring standards don't drop under team pressure
- Work style: structured, process-oriented; high emphasis on written communication (PRFAQs, 6-pagers for proposals)

## Interview Style

### Process Structure
1. Online Assessment (coding + work simulation)
2. Technical Phone Screen (coding, 1 round)
3. Onsite Loop (4–6 rounds):
   - 2x Coding rounds (LeetCode-style, data structures / algorithms)
   - 1x System Design (distributed systems at scale)
   - 1–2x Behavioral rounds (Leadership Principles deep dive)
   - 1x Bar Raiser round (cross-functional, LP-focused)

### Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles (BQ Core)
Customer Obsession, Ownership, Invent and Simplify, Are Right A Lot, Learn and Be Curious, Hire and Develop the Best, Insist on the Highest Standards, Think Big, Bias for Action, Frugality, Earn Trust, Dive Deep, Have Backbone / Disagree and Commit, Deliver Results, Strive to be Earth's Best Employer, Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

**Key insight**: Amazon places MORE emphasis on behavioral/LP questions than most FAANG. "25% of candidates who pass the technical bar are rejected due to behavioral concerns." Each interviewer is assigned 1–3 LPs to probe — stories must map cleanly to specific principles.

### Known BQ Themes
- Customer Obsession: When did you go beyond the spec to serve the user/customer?
- Ownership: When did you take on something outside your scope?
- Invent and Simplify: When did you find a simpler/better way?
- Dive Deep: When did you use data to find a non-obvious root cause?
- Bias for Action: When did you move fast with incomplete information?
- Have Backbone / Disagree and Commit: When did you push back on a decision?

### Technical Expectations
- Clean, well-structured code; communication matters during coding
- System design: distributed systems, scalability, fault tolerance, AWS services knowledge is a plus
- Bar is high for L6 (SDE III): expect deeper LP stories and more complex design rounds

## Customized Pitch Notes

**What to emphasize in "Why Here" section:**
- AWS is the backbone of Singapore's digital economy — express genuine interest in building infrastructure that APAC businesses run on
- The new IOI office as APAC Hub means Singapore engineers have outsized regional influence; tie your ambition to regional/global impact
- Amazon Bedrock and GenAI services: connect your agentic AI experience to the enterprise GenAI wave Amazon is riding
- AWS Summit and community leadership: shows Meta isn't the only one investing in the ecosystem here

**What to watch out for:**
- Every story MUST map to a named Leadership Principle — prepare a story matrix: 1 strong story per LP minimum, ideally 2–3 for the top 8 LPs
- Do NOT say "we" — Amazon interviewers specifically look for "I" ownership language; "we built X" is a red flag
- Frugality LP: have at least one story about doing more with less, cutting scope to deliver faster, or making a cost-conscious tradeoff
- Amazon's RTO is 5 days/week as of 2025 — be prepared to commit to this
- Bar Raiser round is unpredictable — they may go deep on any LP, even ones not listed in the job description
