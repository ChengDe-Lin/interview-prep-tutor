# Stripe — Singapore Research

## Company Overview

- **Singapore office**: One of Stripe's four global engineering hubs (alongside San Francisco, Seattle, and Dublin). ~253 employees in Singapore. All core Stripe functions are represented — product, engineering, partnerships, and operations.
- **Teams based here**: Product and engineering teams working on expanding Stripe's global payments and treasury network, building new products for APAC, and developing underlying infrastructure. APAC-specific payment methods (PayNow Terminal, GoPay, Naver Pay) are built and launched from the Singapore hub.
- **Regulatory standing**: Stripe holds a Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence from MAS, enabling direct partnerships with MAS and EDB.
- **Core products**: Payments API, Stripe Connect, Stripe Terminal, Optimized Checkout Suite, Adaptive Pricing (150 markets), Managed Payments (merchant-of-record), Bridge (stablecoin settlement layer acquired 2025).

## Engineering Culture

- **Tech stack**: One of the world's largest Ruby codebases (20M+ lines). Actively migrating to Java and Go on the backend. Machine Learning / AI tooling increasingly prominent. CI system orchestrates tens of thousands of test suites mixing open-source and novel in-house engineering.
- **Known engineering values**:
  - *Users First*: Every decision anchored to merchant and end-customer impact.
  - *Move with Urgency and Focus*: Bias for action, fast iteration toward best outcome.
  - *Curiosity and Humility*: Prefer investigating to being right; question current practices.
  - *Reliability*: Fault-domain minimization ("reduce the blast radius"); automated, monitored deployments over human babysitting.
  - *Clarity and Operating Rigor*: Written communication-heavy culture, meticulous documentation.
- **Work style**: Hub-and-spoke with Singapore as APAC anchor. Cross-functional embedding (engineers sit close to product and partnerships). Significant autonomy; high bar for written technical reasoning.

## Interview Style

- **Process structure**:
  1. Recruiter call (30 min)
  2. Technical phone screen (60 min) — coding + system design discussion
  3. Super Day (4 rounds): 3 technical rounds + 1 behavioral round
  - Total timeline: 3–5 weeks
- **Technical expectations**: Coding (medium-to-hard LeetCode difficulty), system design (distributed systems, payments reliability), and occasionally API design given Stripe's developer-first product.
- **Known BQ themes**:
  - "Tell me about a time you shipped something that caused production issues — how did you handle it?"
  - "Describe a time you improved a process or system. What was the impact?"
  - "Tell me about a complex cross-team collaboration."
  - "Tell me about a trade-off you made under deadline pressure."
  - "Describe a disagreement and how you resolved it."
  - "Tell me about a bug or incident you owned end-to-end."
- **What interviewers value**: STAR format + a reflection line ("what would I do differently?"). Alignment with Stripe's principles (clarity, bias to action, users first). No hand-waving — specific metrics and decisions required.

## Customized Pitch Notes

- **Why Here emphasis**: Stripe is building the financial internet's backbone from Singapore — the APAC engineering hub is not a satellite; it's a core hub shipping products for the world. The fact that Singapore-based engineers built PayNow Terminal, helped design the Payments Foundation Model, and are integral to the stablecoin/Bridge work makes this a principal engineering role, not a regional support one. That ambition matches ChengDe's track record of owning impact beyond his immediate scope.
- **Angle to highlight**: The agentic AI harness story is a strong opener — Stripe's engineering blog and Payments Foundation Model show they are aggressively AI-native in infrastructure. Framing ChengDe's AI work as "making engineers 10x more productive" resonates with Stripe's bias-for-action and efficiency values.
- **Patent angle**: Two patents signals depth and original thinking — Stripe values engineers who "investigate rather than just execute." Mention briefly in pitch, expand if asked.
- **Watch out for**: Stripe's culture is writing-heavy and rigor-forward. Avoid vague answers. Every claim in behavioral rounds needs a number or a concrete system decision. Prepare to explain *why* you made specific architectural choices, not just *what* you did.
- **Avoid**: Overemphasizing team-building or process work. Stripe values individual engineering output and ownership. Lead with what *you* personally shipped and decided.
