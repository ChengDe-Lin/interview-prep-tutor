## Engineering Culture
- **Tech stack**: C#/.NET (core platform engineering), TypeScript/JavaScript (frontend and VS Code), Python (AI/ML, data science), C++ (low-level systems), Azure cloud-native services (Functions, Service Bus, CosmosDB, AKS), GitHub Actions for CI/CD, Azure DevOps, OpenAI APIs and Azure AI Foundry
- GitHub Copilot is deeply integrated into Microsoft engineering workflows — engineers are expected to be fluent with AI-assisted development
- Azure AI Foundry offers access to 11,000+ models; Microsoft engineers work at the intersection of cloud infrastructure and AI services
- **Growth Mindset (Satya Nadella era)**: The defining cultural shift since 2014 — replacing "know-it-all" culture with "learn-it-all." Engineers are evaluated on curiosity, intellectual humility, and ability to grow, not just current expertise
- **Three leadership principles** (Create Clarity, Generate Energy, Deliver Success):
  - Create Clarity: Simplify complexity; help teams understand what matters and why
  - Generate Energy: Inspire optimism and collaboration; energize rather than drain colleagues
  - Deliver Success: Focus on meaningful, measurable outcomes over activity
- **One Microsoft**: Cross-team, cross-product collaboration is explicitly valued; siloed, territorial behavior is a red flag
- Work style: Hybrid (3 days in office standard); results-focused; strong internal documentation culture
- Microsoft is undergoing a large-scale AI transformation — every product team is embedding Copilot and AI capabilities; engineers with AI experience are in very high demand
- Open-source contribution: Microsoft has become one of the largest OSS contributors (VSCode, TypeScript, .NET); engineers are encouraged to contribute and engage publicly

## Interview Style
- **Process structure**:
  1. Recruiter screen (30 min, background + motivation)
  2. Online assessment (optional, depending on team): coding + work style survey
  3. Phone/Teams screen with hiring manager (45 min, mix of behavioral and technical)
  4. Virtual/Onsite loop: 4–5 back-to-back one-hour rounds:
     - 1 behavioral/values round (growth mindset, leadership principles)
     - 2–3 coding rounds (data structures, algorithms, some OOP design)
     - 1 system design round (senior roles)
     - 1 domain-specific or hiring manager round
- Microsoft weaves behavioral assessment into every technical round — culture fit is evaluated continuously, not just in the dedicated behavioral round
- **Known BQ themes** (anchored to Growth Mindset + Three Leadership Principles):
  - "Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?"
  - "Tell me about a conflict with a teammate. How did you resolve it?"
  - "Describe a time you had to learn a completely new skill to succeed."
  - "Tell me about a time you simplified a complex problem for your team." (Create Clarity)
  - "Describe how you've motivated or energized your team during a difficult period." (Generate Energy)
  - "What's the most impactful project you've delivered? How did you measure success?" (Deliver Success)
- STAR method is the expected response format
- Microsoft explicitly evaluates: Growth mindset, diversity & inclusion awareness, drive for results, cross-team collaboration (One Microsoft)
- Coding: LeetCode medium difficulty; clean code and explanation of approach matter more than raw speed
- System design: Architecture fluency for cloud-scale distributed systems; expect Azure-centric framing

## Customized Pitch Notes
- **Why Here**: Lead with Microsoft's agentic AI work in Singapore (Agentic AI Accelerator, Microsoft Research Asia Singapore lab) and connect to your hands-on agentic AI harness experience. Frame as "I want to be where the agentic AI transformation is being built, not just deployed."
- **Emphasize**: Growth mindset narrative is critical at Microsoft — structure your overall story arc around learning and adapting (NTU academic foundation → rapid promotion → agentic AI self-initiated project → patents). Show the growth trajectory explicitly.
- **Differentiation angle**: Most candidates have coding skills. Your combination of agentic AI hands-on experience + patents + academic excellence + cross-team influence puts you in a rare category for Microsoft's current AI-heavy hiring push.
- **NTU connection**: With Microsoft Research Asia Singapore's 5-year NUS partnership, mention your NTU background and how you see the academic-industry collaboration model as powerful. It positions you as someone who bridges research thinking and engineering execution.
- **Watch out for**:
  - Microsoft interviewers look for intellectual humility — never claim credit without acknowledging team or learning from failure
  - "Growth mindset" is tested for authenticity — prepare a genuine failure story with real learning, not a sanitized non-failure disguised as a weakness
  - System design questions may be Azure-centric — be familiar with Azure services (AKS, CosmosDB, Azure Functions, Service Bus) even if your experience is cloud-agnostic
  - One Microsoft cross-collaboration is a hard check — prepare at minimum 2 stories showing working across team or org boundaries
