# OKX — Singapore Research

## Company Overview

- **Singapore office**: OKX SG PTE. LTD. is a fully licensed, regulated entity in Singapore — not a representative office. OKX received its Major Payment Institution (MPI) licence from MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) for digital payment token services and cross-border money transfer, with the licence fully confirmed by late 2024.
- **Leadership**: Gracie Lin, a former MAS official, was appointed CEO of OKX Singapore — signaling deep regulatory integration and long-term commitment to the Singapore market.
- **Office size / teams**: OKX globally employs ~5,000 staff across 180+ countries. Singapore is one of OKX's primary regulated hubs. Engineering teams in Singapore work on: crypto exchange platform (pro/retail trading), OKX Wallet, Web3 infrastructure, mobile engineering, backend platform, data engineering, and compliance technology.
- **Supernova Program**: A 3-year accelerator for graduates from NUS, NTU, and SMU — 170+ graduates trained in Singapore, covering Product Engineering, Product Management, and Product Design.
- **Core products relevant to Singapore**:
  - OKX Exchange (spot, derivatives trading)
  - OKX Wallet (multi-chain, 100+ blockchains supported)
  - OKX Pay (stablecoin-powered payments via GrabPay SGQR with USDC/USDT, in partnership with StraitsX and Grab)
  - OKX OS (open-source developer toolkit for building on 100+ chains)
  - SGD payment rails (instant SGD deposits/withdrawals via DBS Group)
  - On-chain Earn products for Singapore accredited investors

## Interview Style

- **Process structure** (typical for P5–P7 levels):
  1. Recruiter screen
  2. Technical assessment — DSA coding (60 min) with Senior SWE or Tech Lead
  3. System Design interview (60 min) with Senior Staff Engineer or Architect
  4. Behavioral interview (45 min) with Hiring Manager or Senior Team Member
  - Timeline: 2–4 weeks typical
- **Technical expectations**: Strong DSA (LeetCode medium-to-hard). System design with emphasis on high-throughput, low-latency distributed systems (crypto trading platforms require extreme performance). Familiarity with Java/Spring Boot microservices architecture is expected. Blockchain/Web3 knowledge is a plus, not always required.
- **Known BQ themes**:
  - "Tell me about a time you faced conflict with stakeholders. How did you handle it?"
  - Teamwork and cross-functional collaboration (aligned with "We Before Me")
  - Ownership and delivery ("Get Things Done")
  - Handling ambiguity at scale
  - Past experiences driving technical decisions on large platforms
- **Interview tone**: More structured than Airwallex; process is methodical. Behavioral round is distinct from technical and assessed separately. STAR method expected.
